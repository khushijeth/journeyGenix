Traditional Paintings

Sohrai Painting

Sohrai art, painted on mud walls using natural pigments like red, white, black, and ochre, is a ritualistic tradition celebrated during harvest. The motifs of animals, plants, and geometrical designs symbolize fertility, prosperity, and the close bond between humans and nature. It is a living tradition of the Hazaribagh region.

Khovar Painting

Khovar art, traditionally painted on wedding chamber walls, symbolizes love, fertility, and family bonding. Using black and white contrasts made from natural pigments, it reflects a couple’s union and blessings for a prosperous married life. Geometric, floral, and animal patterns dominate these symbolic artworks.

Handicrafts

Dhokra Art

An ancient 4,000-year-old metal casting technique using lost-wax process, Dhokra art is renowned for intricate brass figurines of animals, tribal deities, and ornaments. Its raw beauty and antique finish make it globally recognized and treasured as heritage art.

Terracotta Art

Crafted from natural clay, Terracotta sculptures and idols often depict gods, goddesses, animals, and everyday tribal life. These clay masterpieces carry earthy textures that highlight Jharkhand’s connection with nature and rural culture.

Tribal Jewellery

Exquisite ornaments made from silver, brass, beads, and natural stones represent the cultural identity of Jharkhand tribes. Each piece narrates heritage, with designs inspired by flora, fauna, and tribal lifestyle.

Local Food Delights

Thekua

A crunchy, sweet snack made with wheat flour, ghee, and jaggery, Thekua is a must-have during Chhath Puja. It carries religious significance and is cherished as a healthy, travel-friendly delicacy that lasts for weeks.

Dhuska

Prepared by deep frying a fermented batter of rice and lentils, Dhuska is a staple breakfast dish in Jharkhand. It is often paired with spicy potato curry or mutton, loved for its crispiness and rich flavor.

Where to Buy

Ranchi Hat Market

A bustling hub for handicrafts and tribal jewellery where you can find authentic Jharkhand treasures at affordable prices.

Hazaribagh Craft Village

World-famous for Sohrai and Khovar paintings, artisans here open their homes for visitors to buy original works.

Jamshedpur Tribal Bazaar

Specializes in Dhokra figurines, terracotta items, and handwoven baskets straight from tribal communities.

Deoghar Local Craft Stalls

Alongside the famous Baidyanath temple, street markets sell traditional jewellery and festive handicrafts.

Chaibasa Weekly Market

Known for tribal ornaments, musical instruments, and bamboo crafts sold by local artisans every week.

Dumka Handicraft Fairs

Seasonal fairs in Dumka display vibrant tribal paintings, textiles, and silver ornaments at reasonable prices.

Palamu Market

A perfect place to explore bamboo crafts, terracotta figurines, and locally made tribal jewellery.

Daltonganj Craft Street

Home to affordable tribal art and handmade utility crafts, perfect for souvenirs and gifting.
